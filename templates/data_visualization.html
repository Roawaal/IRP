Since the UK left the EU, the arrangements for how the UK trades with the EU have changed. The following section will apply data to display the impact of Brexit on UK's overseas trade in agricultural stuff, excluding other goods like material manufactures or fuels.

Moreover, it will pay more attention to annual changes rather than quarterly's, as the seasonal fluctuations in UK agricultural are significant. For example, during the summer months, the UK can grow plenty of its own produce like lettuces and soft berries such as raspberries and strawberries, but when the weather turns colder Britain is forced to rely much more on imports from the EU.
