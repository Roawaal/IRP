Brexit officially took place at 23:00 GMT on 31 January 2020 (00:00 1 February 2020 CET). After 4 years, British people are coming to realize the effects of Brexit on their own lives and livelihoods.

Agriculture plays a significant economic role in the UK, supplying the agri-food sector and engaging in direct sales. The impact of Brexit is sure to have vast implications on the role of agriculture in the UK, as the EU's Common Agricultural Policy has shaped UK farming policies for decades since the 1970s.

This project aims to explore Brexit's wide-ranging effects on agricultral using interactive spatial data analysis technologies to sense trends, analyze the dynamic changes in social media topics and sentiments, understanding public opinion trends.
